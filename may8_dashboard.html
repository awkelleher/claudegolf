Suggested Practice Plan

  1. Strike-first 3W block (15 balls): Tee a ball low and put a tee 2 inches in front of the ball. Make passes that brush both. The goal is shallow, low-point control. Don't worry about distance; chase center contact.
  2. 5-iron alignment block (10 balls): Pick a target line, lay a club on the ground, and hit shots at 80% effort. Prove to yourself that your stock 5i is repeatable. Expect every ball to start within 5 yards of the line.
  3. Mixed gapping block (10 balls): Alternate 5i and 3W. Goal: every 3W must carry 195+ yards (your "good shot" baseline). Walk away from the bay if you can't string 3 in a row.
  4. Diagnostic priority: Next session, ask Trackman for Club Path and Face-to-Path on 3W. The pull + high spin pattern is most likely an over-the-top swing (negative path) with a closed-to-path face. Confirming this lets us prescribe specific drills.
